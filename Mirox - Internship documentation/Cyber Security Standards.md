International organisation for standardization

- **ISO/IEC 27001** :
    
    ISO 27001 certification is a formal recognition that an organization has established, implemented, and maintains an effective Information Security Management System (ISMS) meeting the ISO/IEC 27001 standard requirements.
    
    ISO/IEC 27001 Annex A lists **93 controls** divided into four themes:
    
    - **Organizational (37 controls):** governance, third-party, policies, threat intelligence
    
    - **People (8 controls):** roles, training, awareness
    
    - **Physical (14 controls):** site access, equipment, clear desk policies
    
    - **Technological (34 controls):** access control, encryption, secure coding, monitoring
    
    ## Core Requirements: ISO/IEC 27001 requirements :
    
      
    
    |Clause|Requirement Summary|
    |---|---|
    |4|**Context of the Organization:** Define ISMS scope, identify internal/external issues, stakeholders, and clarify requirements.|
    |5|**Leadership:** Assign leadership, roles, and responsibilities; demonstrate commitment to the ISMS.|
    |6|**Planning:** Identify information security risks through risk assessment, and plan risk treatment; set ISMS objectives.|
    |7|**Support:** Ensure necessary resources, skills, competence, training, awareness, and communication; document and control information.|
    |8|**Operation:** Implement security controls, manage risks, and establish processes for day-to-day ISMS operation.|
    |9|**Performance Evaluation:** Monitor, measure, analyze, and evaluate ISMS performance; conduct internal audits and management reviews.|
    |10|**Improvement:** Address non-conformities and continually improve the ISMS.|
